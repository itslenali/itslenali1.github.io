Challenge

Large environmental displays that engage members of the public must be highly readable and easy to understand. Data-driven displays appear in sports stadiums, transportation facilities, hospitals, or stock trading floors. They create value for these transient groups of individuals by displaying the right data at the right time.

It is critically important for designers to know the data deeply and the needs of users and business stakeholders.

Outcome

1. Examine personas of stakeholders, empathize with them and use them to inform design decisions

2. Analyze a data set using data properties, abstract the data in a model of the current state and generate a model of a future state that serves all stakeholder including two transportation users and one business owner

3. Use data as a design material to help stakeholders achieve their respective goals and support decision making

Data-Driven Design

Designing with data involves designing the logic that governs selection of data and data change. The value of these displays is that they anticipate usersâ€™ needs and change data without requiring interaction from the users. The structure of the data must be informed by a clear understanding of the needs of stakeholders and users in order to satisfy their needs.

Project Overview

Pacific Atlantis Transportation Hub (PATH) is a data-driven display designed to enable flexibility of travel with passengers to choose their mode of transportation and itinerary. The display persists across visual mediums to create a seamless interaction where travelers can find information they need on the larger public display as well as through the mobile phone.
